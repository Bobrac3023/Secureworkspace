!!!EXPLORE THE MODERN WONDER OF THE WORLD!!!

A visit to the Taj is like going back in time. From the word go, the “mesmerizing effect” never leaves you. The Taj speaks volumes of the immense love and affection of a husband towards his wife. A minimum of 3 hours is what I recommend you need to spend exploring the mausoleum. The intricately laid marble works transport you back in time. It makes one wonder how this impressive work of architecture was built with precision without the aid of modern tools and technologies. A good camera should be your able companion as you capture moments for posterity . Remember this mausoleum has been visited by kings, monarchs, presidents and the “who’s who” of the world. The security is extremely robust, so give yourselves ample lot of time to go through the large serpentine queues complete with frisking and physical pat downs. Remember, the Taj is in a high security zone. The local populace is extremely friendly and welcoming, yet some may find it annoying. The nearby Agra Fort is a must see and so is a visit to the local markets to buy souvenirs.

When you visit the "TAJ", it is the experience of a "LIFETIME".

!!! Historical Facts !!!

  • 1.Taj Mahal is distinguished as the finest example of Mughal architecture
  • 2.Designated a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1983
  • 3.Constructed of white marble inlaid with semi-precious stones
  • 4.Commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan

How to get there?

  • 1. Well connected by major highways
  • 2. Served by national and international airports.
  • 3. Three gates in the Taj Mahal: Western,Eastern and Southern.
  • 4. Use a battery-powered golf cart- Parking lot to the Taj Mahal.

"Best time to visit"

  • 1.October to March is the best time period to visit -weather-wise.
  • 2. March, October,November -ideal for photography,cool weather, clear skies, and fewer crowds
